Comparative Adjectives (superiority):

- Form: adjective + -er/-r/-ier (for short adjectives) than or more + adjective (for long adjectives) than 

Superlative Adjectives:

- We use superlative adjectives to compare one thing with all others.

- Form: the + adjective + -est/-st/-iest (for short adjectives) or the most + adjective (for long adjectives)

Comparatives and Superlatives (Inferiority):

Interesting (Interesante):

Comparative: Less interesting than (Menos interesante que)
Superlative: The least interesting (El menos interesante)


Expensive (Caro):

Comparative: Less expensive than (Menos caro que)
Superlative: The least expensive (El menos caro)


Important (Importante):

Comparative: Less important than (Menos importante que)
Superlative: The least important (El menos importante)

Irregular adjectives

These adjectives do not follow the general rule of adding -er /-r /-ier  for the comparative and -est/-st/-iest for the superlative but have irregular forms instead.
